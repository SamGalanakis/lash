01 Identity The logo is Archivo Black with an amber slash mark at -18deg. The slash is the brand — it appears everywhere as a construction mark.
LASH

The Slash
Always at -18deg. Always amber (#e8a33c). It cuts between the S and H, suggesting both a forward slash / and a mark left by impact. In small sizes, the slash alone can serve as the icon.
Registration Marks
Crosshair marks at corners signal precision and alignment. Borrowed from print production — they say "this was built with care." Used on containers and key layout anchors.
Scribe Line
The amber gradient line under the logo. Like a chalk line on a construction surface — it grounds the mark and provides a visual baseline. Fades from sodium to transparent.

05 Elements Visual vocabulary borrowed from construction and print production. Each element has a functional metaphor.
Formwork Seam
Inner border at 8-12px offset. Like the seam left when concrete formwork is removed.
Used on terminal windows and key containers. Signals "this was constructed." Border: var(--border-faint), inset 10-12px from outer edge.
Registration Mark
Crosshair alignment mark. Borrowed from print production. Signals precision.
16-24px, color: var(--ash-mid). Placed at top-left of logo and key layout anchors. Two pseudo-elements: horizontal + vertical 1px lines.
Cut Corner
Chamfered top-right corner. From Whipcrack — suggests a diagonal cut.
16-24px triangle via border trick. Applied to terminal windows. Color matches parent background to create the cut effect.
Score Lines
Diagonal repeating lines at -55deg. Very subtle (0.015 opacity). Background texture on large surfaces. Amber-tinted. Creates directional energy without being visible on casual inspection.
Scribe Line
1px height, gradient from sodium to transparent. Like a chalk line snapped on a surface. Used under logos and as section dividers. Opacity: 0.5.
Dot Separator
······························
Middle dots in var(--ash). Used in terminal output between phases. Replaces dashes or blank lines. Feels like a construction progress marker.
Slash Mark
3px wide, -18deg rotation. The core brand mark. Sodium amber with a soft glow at the tip.
Always at -18deg. Width: 3px (display) or 2px (small). Glow: rgba sodium at 0.2, blur 3-4px. The one element that appears across all contexts.
Grain / Noise
SVG feTurbulence noise, mix-blend-mode: overlay, opacity 0.4-0.5. Applied globally. Adds physical grain to the dark surfaces. Makes flat backgrounds feel like raw material.
06 Principles
01
Built, Not Designed
Every visual element has a construction metaphor. Formwork seams, registration marks, scribe lines. Nothing is decorative — everything refers to a process of making. The aesthetic should feel like it was assembled, not styled.
02
Weight With Warmth
Heavy display type says "this has presence." Warm olive-tinted blacks and sodium amber say "but you can live here." The combination avoids both cold tech sterility and aggressive developer machismo. It's serious and approachable.
03
Meaning Through Scarcity
Color is used sparingly. Sodium amber appears only at brand touchpoints (slash, prompt, accents). Lichen green only for success. Error red only for failures. When a color appears, it means something. Quiet until it matters.
