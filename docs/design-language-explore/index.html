lash · docs · explore three directions, same brief v1.0

design exploration

Seven directions for the lash docs design language, in three rounds. All keep the warm-iron / warm-paper palette family, sodium-amber accent, and the three-voice type system (Big Shoulders Display · Spectral · Chivo Mono). Round 3 (F · G) commits to a single distinctive idea per page — these are the candidates I'd recommend looking at first.

round 6 · split into two treatments — I range as the bold landing page, J page as the toned-down treatment for every other doc page
direction I · round 5 · landing

range

A figure on a peak tip catches rays from one or more suns.

  • Procedural mountain landscape (far/mid/near layers)
  • A dark-robed figure lands on a chosen peak tip
  • Main sun pours a thick sodium ray into her dish
  • 0–2 extra suns may appear; some send slimmer rays
  • Lash C drops to become the page's through-line
  • ↻ regenerate button rolls a new composition
open I (landing)
direction J · round 6 · subpage

page

A thin atmospheric strip — letterhead, not hero.

  • Compressed cover (aspect 16:2): faint distant peaks only
  • No figure, no rays — the full scene lives on the landing
  • Same body stanzas, knots, branches, stations, finial
  • Adds a crumb trail back to the landing
  • Opener title one notch quieter than the hero
  • ↻ regenerate still rolls a new distant range

direction H · round 4

through-line

A single sodium gesture drawn through the page. Accent is the road.

  • Hand-drawn SVG headpiece opens the chapter
  • Mode dispatch is a literal fork in the line
  • Code listings are "stations" along the line
  • Palette stanza widens the stroke for emphasis
  • Principles are knots tied along the line
  • Chapter ends with a calligraphic finial
open H
direction F · round 3

prompt

A typeset transcript of a shell session. Cursor is the active edge.

  • Chapter title is a typed command $ man execution
  • Soft pulsing ▌ cursor in sodium
  • TOC renders as $ ls output
  • Sections open with $ section 04.2 ...
  • Code panels are $ cat with line numbers
  • Palette is a colors --list output
open F
direction G · round 3

plate

The page is an engineering drawing plate.

  • Double-line drawing border + corner ticks
  • CAD-style title block (rev · drawn · sheet 1/1)
  • SVG diagrams with sodium leader-line callouts
  • Cross-refs as → PLATE 05 / §3
  • A live scale bar
  • Modes & principles in proper engineering tables
open G
direction D · new

paper

AMCA-distilled. Cream warm paper, generous space, bold display.

  • Warm cream background (#f5efe0)
  • Massive Big Shoulders chapter mark
  • Mono labels in caps with · separators
  • Soft drop-shadowed swatches & brand-card links
  • No rails, no grids, hairlines + space only
open D
direction E · new

ascii

Soft tech. Paper background, box-drawing chrome, pixel chapter mark.

  • Warm paper + faint 8px dot underlay
  • Pixel chapter mark (VT323)
  • ASCII box-drawing frames on parts
  • Schematic ASCII flow diagrams
  • Numbered [01] principle bullets
open E
direction A

manual

Dark warm-iron printed manual. The page is the artifact.

  • Dark warm-iron palette
  • Folio header with chapter id
  • Hanging marginalia numbers (§ 04.2)
  • Hairline rules carry hierarchy
  • Mode blocks have no fills, no rounded corners
open A
direction B

specsheet

Dark hardware datasheet. Every region has coordinates.

  • Persistent left rail with 04.2 indices
  • Status strip with x/y/z coordinates
  • Type specimens with measurement rules
  • Color tokens as numbered N.01–A.01
  • Crosshair frames + faint 64px grid
open B
direction C

workbook

Dark engineer's field notebook. Open on the bench.

  • Two-column entries with marginalia
  • Run-in subheads (premise., facade.)
  • Faint dot-grid (cross-section paper)
  • Stamp callouts (NOTE, TODO)
  • Numbered footnotes at page end
open C

What's shared. All three render the same sample chapter (Execution / Mode Execution) so you can compare apples to apples. Same palette, same fonts, same content. The differences are in chrome, density, and where the technical apparatus shows through.

Where they sit on a spectrum.

  • Cleanest. A < C < B. The manual aesthetic strips the most ornament; the specsheet adds back coordinate apparatus everywhere.
  • Most dev-inspired. B > C > A. The specsheet leans hardest into mono and CAD-style measurements; the manual stays editorial.
  • Most density. C > B > A. The workbook is two-column with marginalia and footnotes; the manual gives every paragraph room to breathe.

Anti-references honored. No Vercel-style identical cards, no Inter, no gradient text, no side-stripe accents, no glassmorphism, no Linear-flavored polish.
